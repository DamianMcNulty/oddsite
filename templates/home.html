OddProcess

We use an [iterative process], deploying and integrating new functionality frequently and aiming to deliver usable product launches as early and often as possible. We encourage clients to stay closely connected with the daily progress, offering frequent feedback and re-prioritization via [Pivotal Tracker](pivotaltracker.com), our project management tool. The tighter the feedback loop between us, the faster our progress.

In all of our projects we work exclusively with the open web stack: accessible semantic HTML5 markup, unobtrusive Javascript, and advanced CSS3 styling that degrades gracefully in older browsers. Our primary designer, [Eric Meyer](http://github.com/ericam), is a pioneer in the use of these techniques and in building [open-source tools](http://www.oddbird.net/susy/) that make these techniques more accessible to other designers.

On the server side, we are a Python/Django shop, and have extensive Django experience; our primary Python developer, [Carl Meyer](http://github.com/carljm), is a member of the [Django core development team](http://docs.djangoproject.com/en/dev/internals/committers/).
